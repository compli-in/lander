## Part 8: Enforcement Is Not Theoretical

DPDPA introduces a formal enforcement mechanism.

This is not symbolic.

It is designed to act on operational failures.

### The Enforcement Body

The Data Protection Board of India is responsible for:

* Investigating breaches
* Adjudicating non-compliance
* Imposing monetary penalties

Enforcement is triggered by events, not audits.

### What Triggers Enforcement

Typical triggers:

* Data breaches
* Complaints from Data Principals
* Failure to respond to rights requests
* Observed non-compliance during investigations

Enforcement begins when systems fail under real conditions.

### Penalty Structure

Penalties are not flat.

They scale based on:

* Nature of the violation
* Volume of data affected
* Severity of harm
* Repeated non-compliance

Large penalties are possible.

But the critical factor is not size. It is inevitability.

### Intent vs Outcome

DPDPA does not prioritize intent.

It evaluates:

* What happened
* Whether safeguards existed
* Whether obligations were met

Good intentions without system enforcement carry no weight.

### The Breach Reality

Most companies assume breaches are rare.

They are not.

What matters is:

* Detection speed
* Response quality
* Notification compliance

Delayed detection or response escalates exposure.

### Rights Violations

Failure to:

* Provide access
* Correct data
* Delete data

can independently trigger enforcement.

This is not limited to large incidents.

Individual complaints are sufficient.

### The Visibility Problem

Regulators do not rely only on disclosures.

They can:

* Request information
* Examine systems
* Review processes

If systems cannot demonstrate compliance, it is treated as non-compliance.

### Why Companies Get Caught

Common patterns:

* No audit trails
* Inability to trace data flows
* No proof of consent enforcement
* No record of deletion

Without evidence, compliance cannot be defended.

### The Only Useful Model

Design for provability:

* Every action on data must be logged
* Every decision must be traceable
* Every obligation must be demonstrable

If compliance cannot be proven, it is assumed to be absent.

DPDPA enforcement is not about catching violations.

It is about exposing systems that cannot prove they are compliant.
