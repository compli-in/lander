Data compliance refers to the process of adhering to laws, regulations, and standards that govern how organizations collect, store, process, and protect data. ## Why Data Compliance Matters

Organizations face increasing pressure from multiple directions:

- **Regulatory requirements**: GDPR, CCPA, HIPAA, and other regulations carry significant penalties for non-compliance
- **Customer expectations**: Consumers are more aware of their data rights than ever before
- **Business partnerships**: Enterprise clients often require compliance certifications before signing contracts
- **Competitive advantage**: Strong compliance practices build trust and differentiate your brand

## Key Compliance Frameworks

### GDPR (General Data Protection Regulation)

The EU's comprehensive data protection law that applies to any organization handling EU residents' data. Key requirements include:

- Lawful basis for processing
- Data subject rights (access, deletion, portability)
- Privacy by design
- 72-hour breach notification

### SOC 2

A framework developed by AICPA for service organizations, focusing on five trust principles:

1. Security
2. Availability
3. Processing Integrity
4. Confidentiality
5. Privacy

### ISO 27001

The international standard for information security management systems (ISMS), providing a systematic approach to managing sensitive information.

## Building Your Compliance Program

Starting a compliance program can feel overwhelming, but breaking it down into steps makes it manageable:

1. **Assess your current state** - Understand what data you have and how it flows
2. **Identify applicable regulations** - Determine which laws apply to your business
3. **Gap analysis** - Compare your current practices to requirements
4. **Implement controls** - Put policies and technical measures in place
5. **Monitor and maintain** - Compliance is ongoing, not one-time
