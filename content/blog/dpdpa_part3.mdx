## Part 3: Roles That Determine Liability

DPDPA assigns responsibility through roles.

These are not labels. They determine who is accountable when something breaks.

### The Four Roles

**Data Principal**  
The individual to whom the personal data relates.

**Data Fiduciary**  
The entity that decides why and how personal data is processed.

**Data Processor**  
The entity that processes data on behalf of a Data Fiduciary.

**Significant Data Fiduciary (SDF)**  
A Data Fiduciary notified by the government based on scale, sensitivity, and risk.

### The Critical Distinction

Control defines liability.

If you decide:
* What data is collected  
* Why it is collected  
* How it is used  

You are the Data Fiduciary.

Outsourcing processing does not outsource responsibility.

### Where Companies Misclassify Themselves

Common failure patterns:

* Treating vendors as independent fiduciaries when they are processors  
* Assuming processors carry primary liability  
* Not recognizing when they themselves act as processors for clients  
* Ignoring SDF classification triggers  

Misclassification leads to gaps in contracts, controls, and accountability.

### Data Fiduciary Obligations

If you are a Data Fiduciary, you are responsible for:

* Ensuring lawful processing  
* Enforcing purpose limitation  
* Maintaining data accuracy  
* Implementing security safeguards  
* Enabling data principal rights  
* Managing retention and deletion  
* Notifying breaches  

These obligations persist even when data is handled by third parties.

### Data Processor Reality

Processors operate under instruction.

However:

* They must implement security safeguards  
* They must follow contractual obligations  
* Their failures flow back to the fiduciary  

No processor failure is isolated.

### Significant Data Fiduciary (SDF)

SDFs carry additional obligations:

* Appoint a Data Protection Officer  
* Conduct Data Protection Impact Assessments (DPIA)  
* Undergo periodic audits  

SDF status is based on risk, not self-declaration.

### The Vendor Layer

Every processor introduces risk.

Without:

* Defined contracts (DPAs)  
* Clear instructions  
* Audit mechanisms  

The fiduciary loses control.

Vendor management becomes a core compliance function.

### The Only Useful Model

Map every system and vendor to a role.

For each data flow, identify:

* Who decides purpose  
* Who executes processing  
* Who stores the data  
* Who is accountable  

If this mapping is unclear, liability is unclear.

DPDPA does not create new roles. It exposes existing ones.

The mistake is not in misunderstanding definitions.  
The mistake is in not mapping them to reality.
