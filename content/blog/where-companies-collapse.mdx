SOC 2 failures are not caused by missing knowledge.

They are caused by unstable systems.

Most teams know what controls are required.
They cannot sustain them.

## Audit Sprint Behavior

The most common pattern:

* Controls are implemented just before audit
* Evidence is collected in a rush
* Systems are temporarily aligned

After the audit, everything degrades.

This creates cyclical compliance.

Not continuous control.

## Manual Evidence Collection

Evidence is treated as a task.

Failure pattern:

* Screenshots taken manually
* Logs exported on demand
* Spreadsheets maintained by hand

This introduces:

* Gaps
* Inconsistency
* Human error

Manual systems do not scale across audit periods.

## Control Drift

Controls degrade over time.

Failure pattern:

* Access controls loosen
* Monitoring is ignored
* Alerts are not reviewed
* Exceptions accumulate

Without continuous enforcement, controls decay.

SOC 2 Type II exposes this immediately.

## Tool Fragmentation

Controls are spread across tools.

Failure pattern:

* Identity in one system
* Logs in another
* Monitoring elsewhere
* No unified view

No single system shows whether controls are working.

This creates blind spots.

## Ownership Gaps

No clear control ownership.

Failure pattern:

* Security defines controls
* Engineering implements partially
* No one ensures ongoing operation

Controls exist in theory.

Not in practice.

## Evidence Gaps

Even when controls exist, proof is missing.

Failure pattern:

* Logs not retained
* No historical records
* Incomplete audit trails

Without evidence, controls are invalid.

## The Pattern

Across all failures:

* Controls are not embedded into systems
* Evidence is not generated continuously
* Responsibility is fragmented

This guarantees failure under audit.

## The Only Useful Model

SOC 2 requires:

* Controls that run automatically
* Evidence generated continuously
* Visibility across systems
* Ownership of every control

If any of these are missing, compliance is temporary.

SOC 2 does not fail companies.

It exposes systems that were never stable to begin with.
